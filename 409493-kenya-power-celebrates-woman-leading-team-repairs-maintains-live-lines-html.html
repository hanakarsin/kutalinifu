Fortinatah Mandi: Kenya Power Celebrates Woman Leading Team that Repairs, Maintains Live Lines

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Thursday, April 4, 2024

- Fortinatah Mandi Mzee is among the team of technicians who repair and maintain faulty live power lines

- The Kenya Power company described her as an extraordinary and bold woman who goes to great heights to ensure work is done to high standards

- Rising through the ranks to her current position, she is now a team leader of 18 members

Most companies run employee recognition programmes, but often they fall flat.

Many become just another box for managers to check or are seen as elite opportunities for a few, leaving the rest of the workforce feeling left out.

Many managers also fail to adequately express appreciation for their staff, but if they could make a far broader group of employees feel appreciated, the benefits would be considerable.

Employees have revealed that making them feel valued and recognised is not all that complicated. It mostly comes down to many small, commonsense practices.

One company that understands the benefits of appreciating its employees is Kenya Power, where junior staff rise through the ranks to senior positions and are celebrated now and then.

The latest employee to be celebrated for her commitment and love for what she does is Fortinatah Mandi Mzee, who is among the team of technicians who repair and maintain live lines.

Perched high up, she calmly gives instructions to her peers even as she, with the precision of a neurosurgeon, works on a live line.

Despite being metres from the ground and repairing electrical faults with power still flowing on a high voltage line, Mandi is always in her element without fear or second-guessing herself.

According to Kenya Power, using special insulating equipment, the live line team she belongs to works on repairs and maintenance when the line is still live to avoid inconveniencing customers.

The power company described her as an extraordinary and bold woman who goes to great heights, in the literal sense, to ensure that work is done to high standards.

Mandi is the team leader at the Rabai Transmission and says she enjoys the challenges that come with her work.

“When we arrive at a site, we first pray and plan on who will do what on a particular day. Our work requires good planning,” she said.

This kind of undertaking requires audacity and precision, traits that the technician who is now sending waves in a male-dominated field has gained through training and practise.

She joined the company in 2002 after training at the Institute of Energy Studies and Research (IESR) in Electrical Installation and Maintenance.

Of the 40 who were hired, she was the only woman.

Kenya Power said the trailblazer was initially tasked with the repair of transformers, among other duties.

She later joined the live line team and climbed the ladder to become a team leader of 18 members.

“The work is challenging but if you have the passion, it becomes enjoyable,” Mandi said.

She further noted her major inspiration was her late father, who always challenged her never to allow anyone to put her down for being a woman.

“Being a woman should not make you feel inferior at all, women have the potential to achieve as much as they desire”, she added.
