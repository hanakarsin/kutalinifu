Betty Barasa: Primary Investigation Shows Slain Journalist Owned Various Business Companies

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Wednesday, April 24, 2024

- Betty Barasa was murdered at her home in Ngong on the night of Wednesday, April 7

- Investigations showed the journalist was also a renowned businesswoman whose companies worked with the government

- Detectives are now scrutinising her businesses, especially those that worked with the government as they seek to unravel the mystery behind her murder

Besides her work at the Kenya Broadcasting Corporation (KBC), preliminary investigations have shown slain journalist Betty Barasa owned various business companies.

Barasa was shot dead at her home in Ngong on the night of Wednesday, April 7, by a three-man gang that accosted her after her arrival from work.

According to a report by Daily Nation, the journalist was also a renowned entrepreneur who ran businesses some of which worked with the government.

A source disclosed that the deceased and her husband Geoffrey were well-connected and jointly owned a chain of the companies.

Detectives are now scrutinising each and every one of the companies, especially those that were involved with the government; they are seeking to find out if any big payments were made to any of the businesses as they attempt to unravel the mystery behind her murder.

Generous woman

A friend to the late scribe described her as a benevolent person who had a lot of money to spend.

"If you were her friend and had a birthday party, she would finance like 90% of the expenses. She was very generous and everyone at work knew this," the source said.

Further, the sleuths are also analysing Barasa's phone which was dumped near her home after the assassins murdered her.

"Any pattern of communication that would be found on that phone will be the first clue on who her killers are if we manage to find out whether the deceased had any conflict with anyone," one of the sleuths said.

TUKO.co.ke previously reported that the thug who killed her made a mysterious call after finishing her off.

Police officers said even before the assassin killed her, "it seems someone was on a waiting call giving orders.”

The journalist arrived home at around 8.30pm where the gang that was at a nearby construction site waiting accosted her.

The cruel thieves demanded money and other valuables from the journalist as they asked her to lead them to the master bedroom upstairs.

A gunman who had escorted the deceased upstairs argued with her, and soon gunshots rendered the air despite her pleas for her life to be spared.

Betty was shot twice in the head before the robbers took off with her laptop and mobile phone.
