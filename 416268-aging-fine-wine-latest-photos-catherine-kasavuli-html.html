Aging Like Fine Wine: 5 Latest Photos of Beautiful Catherine Kasavuli

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Friday, July 5, 2024
  • On Monday, KBC Channel 1 unveiled its revamped news team consisting of big-name journalists
  • The veteran journalists were Catherine Kasavuli, Tom Mboya, Cynthia Nyamai, Badi Mukhsin, Fred Indimuli, John Kioria, Fayyaz Qureishi, Juma Ballo, Harith Salim, Nancy Onyancha and Shiksha Arora
  • Kasavuli will be hosting a new show dubbed 'Legend Edition' every Saturday on KBC Channel 1
  • Kenyans on social media couldn't hide their excitement after Kasavuli's return and took to social media to warmly welcome her back to the screens

Veteran media personality Catherine Kasavuli is a darling to many, and her return to the screens has sent fans into jubilation. Kasavuli is one of the legends back to Kenya Broadcasting Corporation (KBC).

On Monday, the National Broadcaster unveiled its revamped news team consisting of big-name journalists. Apart from Kasavuli, other names include Tom Mboya, Cynthia Nyamai, Badi Mukhsin, Fred Indimuli, John Kioria, Fayyaz Qureishi, Juma Ballo, Harith Salim, Nancy Onyancha and Shiksha Arora.

#ThisIsKBC has been trending on different social platforms, and Kenyans are convinced that the team will give their counterparts from other Tv stations a run for their money. The State-run Tv station KBC Channel 1 is a free to air channel.

Below are five delightful photos of Kasavuli that have left Kenyans awed.

1. Black doesn't crack. The 59year-old is very beautiful and doesn't look like her age. Her charming smile and gorgeous looks haven't faded away.

2. She retired from the screen 31 years ago. Kasavuli, who is the first female anchor in the country, worked at KBC, KTN and Citizen Tv before retiring in 2015.

3. She is a mother of one. Speaking in a past interview on Citizen Tv Kasavuli said:

"My son had been born in 1981 and was two days shy of celebrating his first birthday when I got a broadcasting job. I was still breastfeeding when I reported for my first job at Voice of Kenya. That is when I began to appreciate broadcasting."

4. She is a mentor and inspiration to many. Several Kenyans have praised her for being a force to reckon, and media personality Njambi Koikai said:

She inspired me as a news anchor and I'm glad to have watched you as a young girl. After 31 years, she's back at BH (Broadcasting House). We, KBC alumni call it Broadcasting House. @catherinekasavuli you have made us dream and hope again. Once a news anchor, always a news anchor. I'm so elated, truly. Your grace, poise, style, class and diction inspired a generation. I know this journey all too well and I thank God that you're back. We love you, Kenya's queen of the screen."

5. Kasavuli will be hosting a new show dubbed 'Legend Edition' every Saturday on the channel.

"It is so wonderful to be back home. There is nowhere like home. After 31 years, what a feeling..." Kasavuli said in a video."

New dawn

The famous Vioja Mahakami show is set for a return on television, and this time, the crop of veteran actors, who made it popular, will also be making a comeback.

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, one of the most celebrated Kenyan actors Gibson Mbugua, aka Prosecutor, revealed that comedian JB Masanduku will also be joining the cast.

"It will be back with the original cast plus younger artistes like JB Masanduku and other upcoming stars," Gibson said.
