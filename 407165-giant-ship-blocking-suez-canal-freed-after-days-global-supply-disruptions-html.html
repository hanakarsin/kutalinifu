Giant Ship Blocking Suez Canal Partially Freed After Days of Global Supply Disruptions

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Monday, April 1, 2024

- This weekend saw a so-called 'supermoon' full moon, which provided higher tidal maximums than usual due to its gravitational pull on the earth's surface

- At least 10 tug boats participated in the refloating operation

A giant container ship blocking the busy Suez Canal has been partially freed almost a week after it ran aground in one of the world’s most important trade paths.

The ship, MV Ever Given and which is almost the size of the State Empire building in New York, has been blocking the vital trade route in the world.

Salvage teams partially refloated the gigantic water vessel in a last-ditch bid to take advantage of the king tide brought on by the supermoon.

This weekend saw a so-called 'supermoon' full moon, which provided higher tidal maximums than usual due to its gravitational pull on the earth's surface.

The salvage teams dislodged the stranded ship early on Monday, March 29, one week after the 1,300-foot-long ship ran aground and choked off global trade, according to maritime services provider Inchcape.

"The MV Ever Given was successfully re-floated at 04.30am, and she is being secured at the moment. More information about the next steps will follow once they are known," Inchcape said in a tweet.

Ten tug boats participated in the refloating operation, the Suez Canal Authority said earlier.

The breakthrough in the rescue attempt came after diggers removed 27,000 cubic meters of sand, going deep into the banks of the canal.

Video clips that have gone viral on social media from the canal show a fleet of tugboats alongside the re-floated Ever Given, blowing their foghorns in a chorus of celebration.

It was not immediately clear how long it would take to reopen the Suez Canal to traffic or to clear the backlog of hundreds of ships awaiting transit.

The ship is owned by Shoei Kisen Kaisha (a shipowning and leasing subsidiary of the large Japanese shipbuilding company Imabari Shipbuilding), and time chartered and operated by container transportation and shipping company Evergreen Marine headquartered in Luzhu District, Taoyuan City, Taiwan.

Ever Given is registered in Panama, and its technical management is the responsibility of the German ship management company Bernhard Schulte Shipmanagement (BSM).

President Abdel Fattah Al-Sisi

The rescue efforts came as Egypt's President Abdel Fattah Al-Sisi ramped up efforts to unblock the single-lane stretch and ordered preparations for the possible removal of some of the ship's 18,300 containers if dislodging efforts failed.

The backlog is one more strain for global supply chains already stretched by the pandemic as the canal is a conduit for about 12% of global trade.

Some ships have already opted for the long and expensive trip around the southern tip of Africa instead of Suez.
