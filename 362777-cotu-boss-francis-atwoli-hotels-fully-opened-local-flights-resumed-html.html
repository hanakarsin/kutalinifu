COTU boss Francis Atwoli wants hotels fully opened, local flights resumed

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Friday, March 1, 2024

- COTU secretary-general, Francis Atwoli, said reopening of the hospitality, aviation and manufacturing sectors will help save many livelihoods from destitution

- He noted resumption of local flights will pump life back in the tourism sector which was adversely affected when local and international flights were suspended

- President Uhuru Kenyatta on June 6 directed Ministry of Transport and aviation players to hasten the development of guidelines that will set the stage for resumption of air travel

- As of Monday, June 15, Kenya had reported 3,727 cases of COVID-19 out of which 1,286 were recoveries and 104 deaths

Central Organisation of Trade Unions (COTU) secretary-general, Francis Atwoli, has urged the government to expedite reopening of businesses that were closed after COVID-19 pandemic hit the country.

Atwoli said among areas that needed to be given priority included manufacturing, aviation and the hospitality sectors.

According to the trade unionists, resumption of some level of normalcy in the sectors would be key in rejuvenating the economy that has been on its knees for three months.

"I want the government to consider opening up local flights. We need people to start flying to and from Kisumu and Mombasa among other areas so that the economy can swing back to life," said Atwoli, adding that the flights will also be pivotal in resuscitating the tourism sector.

Hospitality being the hardest hit sector, the COTU boss said there was need for hotels and restaurants to be allowed to operate up to 7pm for them to be able to get more income.

"The hotels where people are allowed to go take tea and other meals should operate up to 7pm. This will help them recover from the economic shock they have encountered and also enable people to easily access meals before 9pm which marks the start of curfew," he added.

Existing containment measures provided by the Ministry of Health allowed restaurants to operate up to latest 5pm. They require all staff tested for COVID-19 and the sitting arrangement to reflect proper social distancing.

In his speech on June 6, President Uhuru Kenyatta directed the Ministry of Transport and players in the aviation sector to hasten the development of guidelines that will set the stage for resumption of air travel.

Uhuru, however, extended the cessation of movement into and out of expanded Nairobi Metropolitan Area, Mombasa and Mandera counties and ban on international flights for another 30 days.
