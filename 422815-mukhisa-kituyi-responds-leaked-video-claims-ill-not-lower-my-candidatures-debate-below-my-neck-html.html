The 2022 presidential hopeful Mukhisa Kituyi has given a video that allegedly caught him in a compromising state a wide berth.

In June 2021, a video emerged on social media purporting to capture the former UNCTAD secretary-general in a compromising state with a woman while on a vacation in Mombasa.

Speaking to Citizen on Tuesday, August 17, Kituyi dodged questions about the said video insisting he would not lower the debate of his candidature below his neck.

Instead of the purported video, the former Kimilili MP said Kenyans should judge him on the basis of his integrity and how he deals with national challenges.

"Who is Kituyi? What does he stand for? Do I compromise the security and integrity of my country? that is the basis of being judged by Kenyans," he added.

He further dismissed reports that he had linked his political enemies aiming to ruin his reputation to the viral video.

No postponing elections

As previously reported, Kituyi joined the growing list of politicians opposing the postponement of the 2022 General Election.

His sentiments came after The African Court on Human Rights sitting in Arusha, Tanzania gave green light to member states to suspend elections over COVID-19.

Weighing in on the matter, Kituyi insisted that Kenya cannot postpone an election because of a court sitting in Arusha.
