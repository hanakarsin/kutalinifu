The outspoken Kapseret Member of Parliament (MP) Oscar Sudi took a break from his official duties to unwind at a popular club in Eldoret, Uasin Gishu county.

Accompanied by businessman Allan Chesang, Sudi took to the stage at the TMT Lounge and showcased his other 'talent', being a hypeman.

While grooving to Black Uhuru's Statement reggae song, the lawmaker insisted that he was also a great fan of that genre of music.

In a 33 seconds video shared on social media, the all smiling Tanga Tanga allied lawmaker donning a striped shirt is seen skanking to the beats in enjoyment.

In Kenya's political scene, reggae has been used to refer to the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) and its campaigns led by President Uhuru Kenyatta and ODM leader Raila Odinga.

Those against the constitutional amendment report have been perceived not to be reggae music lovers.

However, Sudi dismissed the notion saying, Deputy President William Ruto and his allies were great fans of reggae music and not reggae that is associated with BBI.

"I have heard statements that Ruto and UDA members don't love reggae, We all love reggae music but we dislike that reggae that has been renamed by other people (BBI proponents)," said Sudi.

In a separate story, Uhuru and the former prime minister were spotted on the night of Saturday, February 2, 2020, letting loose and dancing to the tunes of reggae at Carnivore grounds, Nairobi.

The duo freely mingled with Kenyans as legendary UB40 reggae band heated up the dancing floor with cool reggae songs which are understood to be Uhuru's favourite genre of music.

The two leaders boldly showcased their impressive dancing moves not forgetting the signature reggae dance of one finger in the air, which we must say, Uhuru aced.
