Pangani Girls: fees structure, KCSE performance, uniform, location

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Tuesday, May 21, 2024

Pangani Girls is known countrywide because of its impressive performance over the years. The institution seeks to produce holistic citizens who can excel in the highly competitive world. The school develops its students academically, socially, and morally.

As a parent or pupil looking for an academic institution that is a mark of excellence, Pangani Girls is undoubtedly one of the places to go. The institution will mould your child into a responsible person. So, what do you know about about the institution, it location, and KCSE performance?

An overview of Pangani Girls high school

It is a national girls-only public boarding institution. The centre began in 1932 as a girls day school, and it was called Duchess of Gloucester School. Asian students predominantly populated it since Asians were common in the area where the centre was established.

In the beginning, it offered Cambridge and Higher Cambridge education. The system is equivalent to levels O And A education. However, after Kenya attained its independence, the institution changed its name to Pangani Girls.

The centre adopted boarding services in the year 1986. It also started admitting more non- Asian girls. At that time, it was a provincial school. However, considering its impressive performance in the National examinations, the centre was upgraded to a national school in 2011.

Where is Pangani Girls High School located?

The academic centre is located in Pangani sub-location, Kariokor location, Starehe division, Starehe district in Starehe constituency, Nairobi county. You can find it along Juja road at Pangani Girls school fly-over in Nairobi city.

Form one selection criteria and admission procedure

The Ministry of Education does form one placement into the institution. As a national institution, the minimum mark required to join the academic centre is 400. However, guidelines by the Ministry of Education apply.

Pangani High school KCSE performance

Pangani Girls is one of the national schools that have steadily maintained good performance in the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Examination over the years. In 2018, it had a candidature of 282, who managed to score a mean of a B+ with a performance index of 70.281.

In 2019, the centre registered about 270 candidates. The student recorded an impressive mean score of 9.56 (B+) and the institution was ranked thirteenth nationwide. It is also worth noting that the institution produced the best candidates nationally in the 2017 and 2018 KCSE results.

School curriculum

Like all the Kenyan national schools, Pangani Girl's high follows the 8-4-4 curriculum. The subjects taught at the centre are as follows.

Compulsory subjects

  • Mathematics
  • English
  • Kiswahili
  • Physics
  • Chemistry
  • Biology

Optional subjects (student has to choose one)

  • Geography
  • History
  • Christian Religious Education (CRE)

Applied optional subject (student has to choose one)

  • Business Studies
  • Agriculture
  • French
  • Computer studies

For students registering for the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Examination (KCSE), they are allowed to have a total of seven, eight, or nine subjects from the above subjects.

Pangani Girls fee structure

The centre has not disclosed its current fee to the public. Therefore, you will be required to visit the institution to get accurate information on Pangani Girls' High school fees. However, as per the national schools fees structure, institutions in its category are supposed to charge an annual fee of Ksh. 75,789.
