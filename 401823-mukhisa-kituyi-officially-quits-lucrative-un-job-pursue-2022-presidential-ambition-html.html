Mukhisa Kituyi officially quits lucrative UN job to pursue 2022 presidential ambition

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Wednesday, March 20, 2024

- Mukhisa Kituyi was appointed as the seventh secretary-general of UNCTAD and reappointed for a further four-year term in July 2017

- The former MP and Cabinet minister announced on Friday, January 15, that he would be stepping down from his UNCTAD position

- The 65-year-old leader had recently hosted professionals at his home county of Bungoma to jumpstart the 2022 vehicle

The secretary-general of the United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD) Mukhisa Kituyi has stepped down.

Kituyi was previously expected to officially quit the lucrative United Nations job on Monday, February 15. to bring to an end his second non renewable term which ends in September 2020.

The former Kimilili MP was appointed as the seventh secretary-general of UNCTAD and reappointed for a further four-year term in July 2017 which began in September on the same year.

Although there were talks of him running for presidency in Kenya, Kituyi had remained non committal until when he made the official declaration on Saturday, January 2.

The 65-year-old leader had met a team of professionals at his home county of Bungoma to jumpstart the 2022 vehicle to the State House.

“Every challenge is an opportunity for us ,the challenge here is we have a flock without leadership and this is an opportunity for us to lead. We should not confuse political tactic with political purpose, people should not tell us why they want to be president but tell us the reason they want to win power," he told the guests.

Best bet for Kenyans

In 2019, Kituyi received a political boost when Makueni governor Kivutha Kibwana said he was the best bet to deliver Kenyans to the promised land.

The county boss said, based on Kituyi's work experience at the global arena, the former MP had all it takes to be the best presidential bet for Kenya.

"My position is there are three years to go and I am listening to Kenyans. However, the other person I consider for the seat is Mukhisa Kituyi. He is very fresh and has worked at international level thus suitable Kenya needs fresh leadership," he said.
