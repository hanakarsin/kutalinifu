Raila advises parliament against criminalising Ruto's hustler narrative

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Monday, February 19, 2024

- Raila said the constitution of Kenya allows for freedom of speech and association thus proponents of hustler narrative should be allowed to express themselves

- The former premier urged MPs to drop the legislation and instead focus on educating Kenyans about the dangers of propagating the hustler narrative

- The bill to criminalise the hustler versus dynasty narrative is sponsored by Kiambaa MP Paul Koinange through the National Security Committee

- It proposes tough measures that include disqualification from office and a five-year jail term

Opposition leader Raila Odinga has asked the National Assembly to drop the bill seeking to criminalise Deputy President William Ruto hustler narrative.

Raila said although he disagrees with the slogan Ruto's team was using to popularise his 2022 presidential bid, he was aware the constitution allows for freedom of speech and association.

Educate the people

In a statement on Wednesday, February 17, the ODM leader urged MPs to drop the bill and instead focus on educating Kenya about dangers of propagating and embracing such a narrative.

"I would appeal to members of parliament to drop any attempts to legislate against this otherwise deadly slogan.As a country, we fought for, and we must respect freedom of speech and association. We should allow the deputy president and his team to continue with their chosen slogan without any inhibitions. Those of us who see it as dangerous should continue educating our people against falling for it," Raila urged .

The Opposition chief exuded confidence Kenyans would discern the narrative's hidden agenda and reject it.

Great betrayal

Raila's sentiments came barely hours after Siaya senator James Orengo also expressed his reservations on parliament's move to criminalise Ruto's slogan.

The Senate minority leader termed the proposed legislation as "a great betrayal".

“To criminalise what somebody is saying is a great betrayal to the people of Kenya,” warned Orengo.

Tough measures

Kiambaa MP Paul Koinange sponsored the bill to criminalise the hustler versus dynasty narrative through the National Security Committee.

It proposes tough measures that include disqualification from office and a five-year jail term.

"A person who has been dismissed or removed from office for a contravention of sections 62(1) and 62A(1) is disqualified from holding any other state or public office,” the bill reads in part.
